    • Filipino Chicken Recipe

    Filipino chicken dishes are a delightful and integral part of Filipino cuisine. Chicken is one of the most commonly used meats in Filipino cooking, and these dishes offer a wide spectrum of flavors and preparations.
    Chicken is often marinated, braised, or fried to perfection and is seasoned with a blend of ingredients like soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and various herbs and spices. These dishes are a testament to Filipino culinary
    creativity, combining local ingredients and global influences.

    Filipino Pork Recipe

    Filipino pork dishes are a delectable and diverse category of Filipino cuisine. Pork is a versatile meat commonly used in Filipino cooking, offering a wide range of flavors and textures. These dishes showcase
    the rich culinary heritage of the Philippines and are beloved by locals and visitors alike. These dishes are often enjoyed in family gatherings, celebrations, and everyday meals, showcasing the Philippines'
    love for pork in its many delicious forms.

    Pork Sisig

    A popular Filipino dish. It can be considered as a main dish or an appetizer. The composition of this dish is mainly of pig’s parts such as minced pork meat, ears, and face.

    Filipino Dessert Recipe

    Filipino dessert dishes are a delightful and diverse range of sweet treats that reflect the country's rich culinary history. These desserts are known for their unique and delectable flavors, often combining elements
    of Spanish, Chinese, and indigenous influences. Whether served in festive gatherings, simple family dinners, or enjoyed as street food, Filipino desserts bring a sense of warmth and celebration. They are a
    testament to the country's vibrant food culture and are cherished by both locals and those who have had the pleasure of savoring these sweet delicacies.

    Halo-halo

    A beloved Filipino dessert featuring a colorful mix of shaved ice, sweet preserves, jellies, and fruits, topped with leche flan and ube ice cream.

    Click to see Recipe.

    Leche Flan

    A rich and creamy caramel custard, Leche Flan is a classic Filipino dessert known for its silky texture and sweet caramel syrup.

    Click to see Recipe.

    Buko Pandan

    This refreshing dessert combines young coconut strips (buko) and pandan-flavored jelly cubes in a creamy and sweet pandan-infused sauce.

    Click to see Recipe.

    Maja Blanca

    A creamy and coconut-flavored dessert made from coconut milk, cornstarch, and sugar. It's often garnished with latik (coconut curds) or toasted coconut flakes.

    Click to see Recipe.

    Puto Bumbong

    It's made from purple glutinous rice called "pirurutong," which is soaked, ground, and steamed in bamboo tubes. Served with butter, grated coconut, and brown sugar.

    Click to see Recipe.

    Bibingka

    A Christmas favorite, Bibingka is a rice cake made from ground glutinous rice, coconut milk, and often topped with salted duck eggs and cheese.

    Click to see Recipe.

    Cassava Cake

    A Filipino dessert made from grated cassava (a starchy root vegetable), coconut milk, and condensed milk. It's baked to perfection, resulting in a creamy and slightly chewy texture.

    Click to see Recipe.

    Pichi-pichi

    A chewy and gelatinous dessert made from grated cassava, sugar, and coconut, usually covered with grated coconut for added texture and flavor.
